# Playbook 1: Account Compromise & Recovery

**Categories Covered:** Profile Hacking / Identity Theft, Email Hacking, Business Email Compromise (BEC) / Email Takeover

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

### If you can still log in:
1. **Change your password immediately** — use a strong, unique password (12+ characters, mix of letters/numbers/symbols).
2. **Log out of all active sessions/devices** (most platforms have a "log out everywhere" or "manage devices" option in security settings).
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** using an authenticator app (not SMS, if possible).
4. **Check and remove unknown connected apps, devices, or third-party access** linked to the account.
5. **Check recovery email/phone number** — hackers often change this first to lock you out. Revert it to your own details.
6. **Review recent activity/login history** for unfamiliar locations or devices.

### If you are locked out:
1. Use the platform's **"Forgot Password" / Account Recovery** option immediately.
2. Use **official account recovery forms** (Google, Microsoft, Meta, etc. have dedicated hacked-account recovery pages — search only via the official site, not third-party links).
3. If recovery fails, **report the account as hacked** using the platform's official "My account is compromised" form.
4. Alert your **contacts/colleagues** (via another channel — phone, SMS) not to click links or send money/OTPs if messages are coming from your compromised account.

### For Business Email Compromise (BEC) specifically:
1. **Immediately notify your IT/security team** — do not wait.
2. **Disconnect the affected device from the network** (unplug Wi-Fi/Ethernet) if malware is suspected.
3. **Freeze any pending financial transactions** tied to the compromised email (wire transfers, invoice payments).
4. **Alert your bank** immediately if any fraudulent transaction was initiated.
5. **Reset credentials for ALL linked accounts** (email is often the master key to other accounts).
6. **Check email forwarding/auto-reply rules** — attackers often set up hidden forwarding rules to silently monitor your inbox. Remove any you didn't create.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

Collecting evidence properly is critical for reporting and any potential legal/insurance action. Save everything **before** making major changes where possible (e.g., screenshot suspicious activity before deleting it).

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Screenshots** | Login alerts, suspicious emails/messages, unauthorized posts, ransom/extortion messages, changed profile details |
| **Login history** | Download/export account activity logs (IP addresses, device info, timestamps) from account security settings |
| **Email headers** | Save full email headers (not just the message) — they reveal sender IP and routing info, crucial for tracing |
| **Financial records** | Bank statements, transaction IDs, screenshots of fraudulent transfers (for BEC cases) |
| **Communication logs** | Any chats/calls with the attacker, ransom demands, or impersonation messages sent to your contacts |
| **URLs/links** | Copy the exact phishing link or fake site URL (don't click again) |
| **Timestamps** | Note the date/time you first noticed the compromise and each subsequent step taken |
| **Original account details** | Username, registered email/phone, account creation date (helps prove ownership during recovery) |

**Tip:** Keep a simple text log: *"What happened, when I noticed it, what I did, and when."* This timeline is often requested by both platforms and police.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Report to the Platform (Do this first, immediately)
- **Google/Gmail:** [g.co/recoveraccount](https://g.co/recoveraccount) — official hacked account recovery
- **Microsoft/Outlook:** [account.live.com/acsr](https://account.live.com/acsr) — compromised account form
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram):** [facebook.com/hacked](https://facebook.com/hacked) or [help.instagram.com/hacked](https://help.instagram.com/hacked)
- **X (Twitter):** [help.twitter.com/forms/hacked](https://help.twitter.com/forms/hacked)
- **LinkedIn:** Report via LinkedIn Help Center → "Hacked Account"

### Step 2 — Report to National Cybercrime Authorities (India)
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint under "Report Other Cyber Crime." Provide evidence collected above (screenshots, headers, timeline).
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** (toll-free, for financial fraud linked to BEC)
- **Local Police Cyber Cell:** File an FIR if financial loss occurred or identity theft is severe — required for legal/insurance follow-up.

### Step 3 — Report to CERT-In (for organizations/BEC cases)
- **CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team):** [cert-in.org.in](https://www.cert-in.org.in) — Report the incident, especially for business-related compromises. Email: incident@cert-in.org.in

### Step 4 — Check & Secure Your Mobile Number (Sanchar Saathi)
Identity theft often involves SIM cards being fraudulently issued in your name (used to bypass OTP-based 2FA) or your number being SIM-swapped to take over linked accounts.
- **Sanchar Saathi Portal:** [sancharsaathi.gov.in](https://www.sancharsaathi.gov.in) — Government of India (DoT) portal.
  - Use **"Know Your Mobile Connections" (TAFCOP)** to check how many SIMs are registered under your name/ID and disconnect any you don't recognize.
  - Use **"Chakshu"** on the same portal to report suspicious calls/SMS/WhatsApp messages used for the fraud (phishing, impersonation).
  - If your phone itself was stolen/lost (common in identity-theft cases), use **"Block Your Lost/Stolen Mobile"** — you'll need a police complaint (FIR) copy, ID proof, and IMEI number.

### Step 5 — Notify Affected Parties
- Inform your bank if financial details were exposed.
- Inform your employer/IT team (for BEC).
- Inform contacts who may have received phishing messages from your compromised account.

### Step 6 — International/Additional Reporting (if applicable)
- **IC3 (FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center)** — if attacker/transaction is US-linked: [ic3.gov](https://www.ic3.gov)
- **Action Fraud (UK)** — if UK-linked: [actionfraud.police.uk](https://www.actionfraud.police.uk)

## 4. Helpful Recovery & Support Resources

| Need | Resource |
|---|---|
| **Identity theft monitoring/guidance** | [identitytheft.gov](https://www.identitytheft.gov) (US-focused but useful step-by-step guidance) |
| **Check if your data was leaked** | [haveibeenpwned.com](https://haveibeenpwned.com) — check if your email/password appeared in known breaches |
| **Phishing site takedown** | Report the phishing URL to [Google Safe Browsing](https://safebrowsing.google.com/safebrowsing/report_phish/) so it gets blacklisted |
| **Impersonation profile removal** | Report directly via the platform's "Impersonation" report option (Meta, Instagram, X all have dedicated impersonation forms separate from general hacking reports) |
| **Credit/financial fraud monitoring (India)** | Contact your bank's fraud helpline and consider freezing credit via CIBIL/credit bureau alerts |

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Change password + enable 2FA
2. ✅ Log out all sessions / revoke unknown app access
3. ✅ Restore recovery email/phone
4. ✅ Screenshot & save all evidence (headers, logs, messages)
5. ✅ Report to platform → cybercrime.gov.in → 1930 helpline → CERT-In (if business) → Sanchar Saathi (check/secure mobile number)
6. ✅ Notify bank/contacts/employer as relevant
7. ✅ Monitor accounts for further suspicious activity for the next few weeks
