# Playbook 11: Critical & Organized Cybercrime

**Categories Covered:** Human/Drug Trafficking, Online Gambling, Cyber Terrorism, Ransomware, Other Organized Cybercrime

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

### If you've encountered trafficking, terrorism-related content, or organized crime activity online:
1. **Do not engage, respond, or attempt to investigate it yourself** — this can endanger you and compromise any investigation.
2. **Do not share, forward, or download** any such content, even to "report" it elsewhere — this can itself constitute an offense and risks wider spread.
3. **Screenshot/preserve the content immediately** (profile, post, chat, payment request) without interacting further.
4. **If you or someone you know is in immediate danger** (e.g., trafficking victim, threat of violence), **call 112 (emergency) immediately** — this overrides all other steps.
5. **Block the account/contact** only after you've preserved evidence — don't alert them by engaging.

### If you encounter illegal online gambling platforms:
1. **Stop all transactions with the platform immediately** if you're a user — many such platforms are fronts for money laundering and may suddenly block withdrawals.
2. **Save transaction records and platform details** before reporting.
3. **Do not recruit others or share referral links** for such platforms, even unknowingly — this could implicate you in the offense.

### If you are a victim of Ransomware:
1. **Disconnect the infected system from the network immediately** (unplug Ethernet/disable Wi-Fi) to stop the ransomware from spreading to other connected systems/drives.
2. **Do NOT pay the ransom** — payment doesn't guarantee data recovery, funds organized crime, and may violate sanctions laws depending on the recipient.
3. **Do not power off the system** if possible — this preserves volatile memory evidence that may help with decryption/forensics. Just isolate the network connection.
4. **Photograph the ransom note/screen** displayed before doing anything else.
5. **Check if a free decryptor exists** for the specific ransomware strain via "No More Ransom" (see Resources below) before considering any other option.
6. **Restore from offline/clean backups** only after the infected system is fully wiped and the entry vulnerability is identified and patched.
7. **Notify your IT/security team or a professional incident response firm immediately** — ransomware recovery is complex and mistakes can permanently destroy recovery options.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Screenshots (trafficking/terrorism/gambling content)** | Profile/page URL, post content, timestamps — capture without downloading/forwarding the actual harmful media itself if it's illegal content (e.g., CSAM) |
| **Ransom note** | Photograph/screenshot the full ransom message, including any wallet address, contact email, and ransomware strain name if mentioned |
| **Encrypted file samples** | Keep a few encrypted file samples (don't delete) — needed for ransomware strain identification and potential decryption |
| **System logs** | Network logs, endpoint detection logs showing the infection vector and spread pattern |
| **Payment/transaction records (gambling)** | Screenshots of deposits, withdrawal attempts, account balance shown by the platform |
| **Communication records** | Any chat, recruitment message, or instructions received related to the organized activity |
| **Timeline log** | Discovery time, isolation/containment actions taken, and reporting times — critical for both law enforcement and any regulatory deadlines |

**Important:** For trafficking or child exploitation content specifically, **do not store or forward the illegal content itself** on your own devices — report the location/link/profile directly to authorities and let them handle retrieval. Storing such content yourself can carry legal risk even with good intent.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Immediate Danger? Call Emergency Services First
- **Dial 112** if there is an immediate threat to someone's life or safety (trafficking victim, violence threat, terrorism-related danger).

### Step 2 — Report to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
- **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** — Use the **"Report Child Sexual Abuse Material/Sexually Explicit Material"** category for trafficking/exploitation content, or **"Report Other Cyber Crime"** for gambling/ransomware/general organized crime. This portal directly routes sensitive content to law enforcement, including anonymous reporting options.

### Step 3 — Dial 1930 (for Ransomware/Gambling with Financial Loss)
- If money was paid (ransom or gambling-related fraud), report immediately for the best chance of tracing/freezing funds.

### Step 4 — Report to CERT-In (Mandatory for Ransomware in Organizations)
- **[cert-in.org.in](https://www.cert-in.org.in)** — Email: incident@cert-in.org.in. Ransomware attacks are a mandatory reporting category under CERT-In's directions, generally within 6 hours of detection for applicable entities. CERT-In may also have specific guidance for the ransomware strain involved.

### Step 5 — Report Trafficking-Specific Content
- **National Human Rights Commission (NHRC):** [nhrc.nic.in](https://nhrc.nic.in) — for human trafficking complaints.
- **Anti Human Trafficking helpline:** Dial **1098** (Childline, also handles child trafficking) or report via cybercrime.gov.in's dedicated category.

### Step 6 — Report Terrorism-Related Content
- **National Investigation Agency (NIA):** [nia.gov.in](https://www.nia.gov.in) — for cyber terrorism content/threats.
- Most social platforms also have a dedicated **"Report Terrorism"** option separate from general content reporting — use this for faster takedown alongside official reporting.

### Step 7 — File FIR at Local Police Cyber Cell / Economic Offences Wing
- Necessary for ransomware (extortion), illegal gambling operations, and any organized crime case requiring formal investigation.

## 4. Helpful Resources

| Need | Resource |
|---|---|
| **Free ransomware decryption tools** | [No More Ransom](https://www.nomoreransom.org) — a joint initiative by Europol and cybersecurity companies offering free decryptors for many known ransomware strains |
| **Report CSAM/exploitation content anonymously** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) anonymous reporting option; also [NCPCR's POCSO e-Box](https://ncpcr.gov.in/ebox/) |
| **Human trafficking support** | [nhrc.nic.in](https://nhrc.nic.in); Childline **1098** |
| **CERT-In ransomware advisories** | [cert-in.org.in](https://www.cert-in.org.in) — published guidance specific to active ransomware campaigns |
| **Illegal gambling platform verification** | Check state-specific gambling laws via official state government portals; most online gambling/betting is restricted or illegal in many Indian states |
| **Identify ransomware strain** | [ID Ransomware](https://id-ransomware.malwarehunterteam.com) — upload the ransom note/encrypted file sample to identify the specific strain |

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Call 112 immediately if there's danger to life/safety
2. ✅ Preserve evidence — screenshot, don't forward/download harmful content yourself
3. ✅ Isolate infected systems immediately for ransomware (don't power off, don't pay)
4. ✅ Check "No More Ransom" for a free decryptor before any other ransomware action
5. ✅ Report on cybercrime.gov.in (use the correct specific category) → dial 1930 if money lost
6. ✅ Report to CERT-In within 6 hours for organizational ransomware (mandatory)
7. ✅ Use specialized authorities for trafficking (NHRC/1098) or terrorism (NIA) content
8. ✅ File FIR with local police cyber cell / Economic Offences Wing
