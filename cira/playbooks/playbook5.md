# Playbook 5: Employment & Relationship Scams

**Categories Covered:** Job Fraud, Matrimonial Fraud

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

### Job Fraud (fake job offers, advance fee scams, fake recruiters):
1. **Stop all further payments immediately** — legitimate employers never ask for money for "registration," "training kits," "security deposit," or "visa processing."
2. **Do not share more personal documents** (Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, passport copy) with the recruiter if you suspect fraud.
3. **Contact your bank immediately** to attempt to stop/reverse any payment made via UPI/bank transfer if it was very recent.
4. **Save the job offer letter, recruiter's contact details, and company name** before they can be deleted/blocked.
5. **Check the company's legitimacy** — search the official company website/HR contact directly (not the number given by the recruiter) to confirm if the job offer is real.
6. **If you've already shared bank/card details**, monitor your account closely and consider freezing the card temporarily.

### Matrimonial Fraud (fake matrimonial profiles, romance scams, fraudulent marriage proposals for money):
1. **Stop sending any more money immediately** — common patterns include emergency hospital bills, customs/visa fees, or "stuck abroad" stories.
2. **Do not share intimate photos/videos** if the relationship has turned suspicious — these can be used for blackmail later.
3. **Reverse image search their photos** to check if the profile is fake/stolen from someone else.
4. **Stop video calls** if you suspect the person is using a fake identity or deepfake/filtered appearance.
5. **Inform a trusted family member or friend** about the situation — romance scam victims are often isolated deliberately by the scammer, so breaking that isolation is important.
6. **Preserve all chat history** before blocking the person — don't delete the conversation even if you want to.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Job offer letter / matrimonial profile** | Screenshot full content — company name, designation, salary, fake profile photos, bio details |
| **Payment proof** | Screenshots of UPI/bank transfer receipts, transaction IDs, amount, date, recipient account/UPI ID |
| **Communication records** | Full chat history (WhatsApp, email, matrimonial site messages) — don't delete even after blocking |
| **Recruiter/scammer contact info** | Phone number, email address, fake company name/website used |
| **Bank account details of scammer** | Account number/UPI ID money was sent to (critical for tracing and freezing the account) |
| **Photos/videos shared** | If used for blackmail, store securely and report directly to police/cybercrime — don't forward widely |
| **Timeline log** | Date of first contact, date(s) of payments, date you realized it was fraud |

**Tip:** For matrimonial fraud, also save details of **how you met them** (which app/site/platform) — this helps the platform investigate and warn other users.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Call Your Bank Immediately (if payment was just made)
- Use your bank's official fraud helpline to attempt to **stop or reverse the transaction** — speed matters, especially within the first few hours.

### Step 2 — Dial the Cyber Crime Helpline
- **Dial 1930** — India's cybercrime financial fraud helpline, for fastest action on freezing the fraudster's account before money is withdrawn.

### Step 3 — File a Complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
- **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** — File under "Financial Fraud" (for job/matrimonial fraud involving payment) or "Women/Child Related Crime" if applicable (for romance scams targeting women, blackmail with intimate content).

### Step 4 — Report to the Platform
- **Job portals** (Naukri, LinkedIn, Indeed): Report the fake job posting/recruiter profile directly via "Report this Job" option.
- **Matrimonial sites** (Shaadi.com, Jeevansathi, Bharat Matrimony): Report the fake profile via the platform's "Report Profile" feature — most matrimonial sites have a dedicated fraud team.

### Step 5 — Report to Sanchar Saathi (if number was used for the scam)
- **Chakshu (Sanchar Saathi):** [sancharsaathi.gov.in](https://www.sancharsaathi.gov.in) — report the scammer's phone number for investigation/disconnection.

### Step 6 — File an FIR at Local Police Cyber Cell
- Important for larger financial losses or when blackmail with intimate content is involved (extortion is a serious criminal offense).

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Stop sending money immediately
2. ✅ Verify the company/profile independently before any further action
3. ✅ Save all chats, payment proofs, and contact details
4. ✅ Call your bank to attempt transaction reversal
5. ✅ Dial 1930 → file complaint on cybercrime.gov.in
6. ✅ Report fake profile/listing to the platform (job site/matrimonial site)
7. ✅ File FIR if large financial loss or blackmail is involved
8. ✅ Talk to someone you trust — don't isolate yourself
