# Playbook 4: Phishing & Social Engineering

**Categories Covered:** Phishing, Vishing

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

### If you clicked a phishing link or shared details (but no money lost yet):
1. **Do not enter any more information** on the suspicious site/form — close it immediately.
2. **Change passwords immediately** for any account whose details you may have entered (use a different, secure device if possible, in case the original device is compromised).
3. **Enable 2FA** on all important accounts (banking, email, social media) right away.
4. **Run a malware/antivirus scan** on the device used, in case the link installed malicious software.
5. **Disconnect from Wi-Fi/network** temporarily if you suspect malware installation, until scanned.

### If you shared OTP/card details and suspect money is at risk (Vishing/Phishing):
1. **Call your bank's official fraud helpline immediately** (use the number on the back of your card or official bank website — never a number given by the caller) to **block your card/account**.
2. **Do NOT share any further OTPs** with anyone, even if they claim to be "verifying" or "reversing" the fraud — banks/RBI never ask for OTP over a call.
3. **Initiate a transaction dispute** with your bank if money has already been debited.
4. **Check linked UPI apps** (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) — temporarily disable/freeze them from the app's security settings if compromised.

### If it was a Vishing call (fraudulent phone call):
1. **Hang up immediately** — don't continue the conversation or follow their instructions (e.g., "install this app for verification").
2. **Do not install any app** they ask you to (often disguised as "AnyDesk," "TeamViewer," or fake banking apps used for remote access fraud).
3. **If you already installed a remote-access app**, uninstall it immediately and turn off mobile data/Wi-Fi until you've scanned the device.
4. **Note down the caller's number** (even if it looks spoofed) for reporting purposes.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Phishing email/SMS** | Screenshot the full message including sender address/number, and save the original email file (.eml) if possible — headers reveal the real source |
| **Phishing website URL** | Copy the exact link (don't click again) — needed to report for takedown |
| **Vishing call details** | Caller's number, time/date of call, what they said, what app/info they asked for — write this down immediately while memory is fresh |
| **Bank/transaction records** | Screenshots of the fraudulent transaction, transaction ID, SMS alerts from bank |
| **App installation history** | If a remote-access app was installed, note the app name and installation time |
| **Communication trail** | Any follow-up messages/calls from the scammer trying to extract more info or money |
| **Timeline log** | Exact sequence: when you got the call/message, what you did, when you noticed something was wrong, when you reported it |

**Tip:** The faster you report a financial vishing/phishing scam (within minutes to hours), the higher the chance banks can freeze/reverse the transaction before it's withdrawn.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Call Your Bank/Financial Institution IMMEDIATELY (if money is involved)
- Use the **official customer care number** from your bank's card/passbook/website — block the card, freeze the account, and request a transaction dispute/chargeback.

### Step 2 — Call the Cyber Crime Helpline (within golden hours)
- **Dial 1930** — India's national cybercrime financial fraud helpline. This is specifically designed to help **freeze fraudulent transactions quickly** if reported fast. This is the single most time-critical step for financial phishing/vishing.

### Step 3 — File a Complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
- **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** — File under "Financial Fraud" category. Include all evidence (screenshots, transaction IDs, phishing link/number).

### Step 4 — Report to Sanchar Saathi (Chakshu) for the fraudulent number/call
- **[sancharsaathi.gov.in](https://www.sancharsaathi.gov.in)** → **Chakshu** service — report the specific phone number/SMS used for vishing/phishing so it can be investigated and the connection potentially disconnected.

### Step 5 — Report the Phishing Site/Link for Takedown
- **Google Safe Browsing:** [safebrowsing.google.com/safebrowsing/report_phish](https://safebrowsing.google.com/safebrowsing/report_phish/) — gets the site flagged/blocked in browsers.
- **Report to the hosting/domain provider** via WHOIS lookup if the above doesn't apply.
- If it impersonates a specific bank/company, **forward the phishing email/SMS to that company's official abuse/security email** (most banks have a dedicated phishing report email, e.g., report.phishing@[bankname].com — check their official website).

### Step 6 — Report to CERT-In (for awareness/large-scale phishing campaigns)
- **[cert-in.org.in](https://www.cert-in.org.in)** — especially relevant if it's a large-scale or organizational phishing campaign. Email: incident@cert-in.org.in

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Stop — don't share more OTP/info, hang up/close the page immediately
2. ✅ Call your bank to block card/account if money is at risk
3. ✅ Dial 1930 immediately (golden hours matter for fund recovery)
4. ✅ File complaint on cybercrime.gov.in with all evidence
5. ✅ Report the number via Sanchar Saathi's Chakshu
6. ✅ Report the phishing link to Google Safe Browsing for takedown
7. ✅ Change passwords + enable 2FA on all affected accounts
8. ✅ Scan device for malware if a link was clicked or app installed
