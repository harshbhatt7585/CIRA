# Playbook 2: Impersonation & Identity Misuse

**Categories Covered:** Fake Profile, Impersonating Email, Cheating by Impersonation

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

1. **Do not engage or argue** with the fake profile/impersonator directly — this can escalate the situation or alert them to delete evidence.
2. **Take screenshots immediately** of the fake profile/email before it can be taken down or edited by the impersonator (evidence first, action second).
3. **Report the profile/email to the platform** using the in-app "Report Impersonation" feature (not the general "report" option — platforms handle impersonation reports differently and faster).
4. **Alert your contacts** — post a warning on your real account, or message close contacts directly, that a fake profile/email is impersonating you and they shouldn't trust messages from it.
5. **If money was requested in your name** (cheating by impersonation), immediately inform anyone who may have been contacted, and alert your bank if you suspect linked financial fraud.
6. **Secure your real accounts** — impersonation often starts with stolen photos/info from your real profile. Tighten privacy settings (who can see your photos, friends list, personal info).
7. **If a fake email domain mimics your company** (e.g., yourname@company-support.com instead of @company.com), notify your IT/security team immediately so they can alert employees and clients.

---

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Screenshots of fake profile/email** | Full profile page (photo, bio, posts), sender email address, full email body and headers |
| **Profile/Account URL** | Copy the exact link/URL of the fake profile before reporting (platforms may take it down, so save proof first) |
| **Messages sent by impersonator** | Screenshot any chats, especially ones asking for money, personal info, or making false claims |
| **Victim communications** | If others were cheated/deceived, collect their screenshots and statements too — strengthens the case |
| **Financial proof (if cheating occurred)** | Transaction screenshots, UPI/bank transfer IDs, payment requests made in your name |
| **Timeline log** | When you first discovered it, what actions you took, and when |
| **Witness statements** | If friends/colleagues/clients were contacted by the fake account, ask them to save and share their screenshots |

**Tip:** Always screenshot with date/time visible (don't crop it out) — this helps establish a timeline for investigators.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Report to the Platform (Fastest takedown route)
- **Facebook:** [facebook.com/help/contact/295309487309948](https://www.facebook.com/help/contact/295309487309948) — "Report Impersonation"
- **Instagram:** [help.instagram.com](https://help.instagram.com) → Report → "Pretending to be someone"
- **X (Twitter):** [help.x.com/forms/impersonation](https://help.x.com/forms/impersonation)
- **LinkedIn:** Profile → Report/Block → "Fake profile"
- **Fake Email:** Report to the email provider (Gmail/Outlook abuse report) and report as phishing/impersonation if it mimics a business

### Step 2 — Report to National Cybercrime Authorities (India)
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File under "Women/Child Related Crime" (if impersonation involves a woman/child victim) or "Report Other Cyber Crime." This applies to impersonation under IT Act Section 66D and cheating by personation under IPC/BNS.
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** for financial fraud linked to impersonation/cheating.
- **Local Police Cyber Cell:** File an FIR — especially important if money was cheated or reputational/business harm occurred, as this is required for legal action.

### Step 3 — Report to Sanchar Saathi (if phone number misused)
- **Sanchar Saathi Portal:** [sancharsaathi.gov.in](https://www.sancharsaathi.gov.in) — Use **Chakshu** to report if your phone number is being used/spoofed for impersonation calls/SMS, or **TAFCOP** to check if SIMs are fraudulently issued in your name.

### Step 4 — Notify Affected Third Parties
- Inform friends, family, colleagues, or clients who may have interacted with the fake profile.
- If it's a business impersonation, notify your company's legal/PR/IT team so they can issue a public clarification if needed.

### Step 5 — Legal Notice (for serious/business cases)
- Consider sending a **legal notice/cease-and-desist** via a lawyer to the platform demanding takedown, especially for repeated or large-scale impersonation affecting business reputation.

## 4. Helpful Resources

| Need | Resource |
|---|---|
| **Verify a domain/email legitimacy** | [Google Postmaster Tools](https://postmaster.google.com) or [MXToolbox](https://mxtoolbox.com) to check suspicious email headers/domains |
| **Reverse image search** (check if your photos are used elsewhere) | [Google Images](https://images.google.com) or [TinEye](https://tineye.com) |
| **Report fake profile to multiple platforms at once** | [StopNCII.org](https://stopncii.org) (if impersonation includes misuse of your photos/images — works with partner platforms) |
| **Trademark/business impersonation** | [WIPO](https://www.wipo.int) or domain registrar's abuse report form, if a fake domain is impersonating your business |

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Screenshot the fake profile/email BEFORE reporting (evidence first)
2. ✅ Report impersonation directly to the platform
3. ✅ Warn your real contacts/network about the fake account
4. ✅ Secure your real account's privacy settings
5. ✅ File complaint on cybercrime.gov.in → dial 1930 if money was involved
6. ✅ Use Sanchar Saathi (Chakshu/TAFCOP) if your number was misused
7. ✅ File FIR if financial loss or serious reputational harm occurred
