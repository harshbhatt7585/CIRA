# Playbook 6: Banking & Payment Fraud

**Categories Covered:** UPI Fraud, Banking Fraud, Wallet Fraud, AEPS (Aadhaar Enabled Payment System) Fraud

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

1. **Call your bank's official fraud helpline IMMEDIATELY** (number from the back of your card/passbook/official website — never a number given to you by a caller) to:
   - **Block your debit/credit card**
   - **Freeze/suspend your bank account or UPI ID** if multiple unauthorized transactions are occurring
2. **Dial 1930** (national cybercrime financial fraud helpline) **within the first few hours** — this is the most time-critical step, as funds can sometimes be frozen before withdrawal if reported fast.
3. **Do not share any more OTPs, PINs, or CVVs** with anyone — even someone claiming to be from the bank, RBI, or "fraud department." Banks never ask for these over a call.
4. **Check and disable UPI apps** (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM) from your phone's security settings if you suspect your UPI PIN is compromised — you can temporarily disable from the app itself.
5. **For wallet fraud** (Paytm, Amazon Pay, etc.): Log in and immediately change your wallet PIN/password, and check linked bank accounts for further unauthorized access.
6. **For AEPS fraud** (Aadhaar-linked fingerprint-based withdrawals without your knowledge):
   - **Lock your Aadhaar biometrics immediately** via the UIDAI portal/app — this is critical since AEPS fraud uses your fingerprint data without physical access to your card or phone.
   - Contact your bank to flag suspicious AEPS transactions on your account.
7. **Request a transaction dispute/chargeback** from your bank for any fraudulent debit — this must usually be initiated within a specific window (often 3 days for "zero liability" protection under RBI rules), so don't delay.
8. **Change your net banking/UPI app password** from a different secure device.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Transaction SMS/alerts** | Screenshot every fraud alert SMS/email from the bank, showing amount, time, and reference number |
| **Bank statement** | Download/screenshot the statement section showing the fraudulent transaction(s) |
| **Transaction ID / UTR number** | Critical for tracing — found in the SMS alert or bank app transaction history |
| **Recipient account/UPI ID** | The account/UPI handle the money was transferred to (needed for tracing and freezing) |
| **Call/message records (if vishing-linked)** | Screenshot any call/message that led to the fraud, including the number used |
| **AEPS-specific evidence** | Screenshot of AEPS transaction alert, and note which bank/branch/agent the withdrawal was reportedly made through |
| **Communication with the bank** | Save complaint reference numbers, emails, and screenshots of your dispute/complaint submission |
| **Timeline log** | When you noticed the fraud, when you called the bank, when you reported to 1930/cybercrime.gov.in — exact timestamps matter for "golden hour" fund recovery |

**Tip:** Always note your **complaint/ticket reference number** every time you call your bank or file a report — you'll need it for follow-ups.

---

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Call Your Bank's Fraud Helpline IMMEDIATELY
- Block card/account, request transaction freeze, and initiate a formal dispute. Get a **complaint reference number**.

### Step 2 — Dial 1930 (National Cyber Crime Financial Fraud Helpline)
- This is the **single most important and time-critical step**. The 1930 helpline is directly linked to the banking/payment ecosystem to flag and freeze fraudulent accounts quickly, often within the so-called "golden hour."

### Step 3 — File a Complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
- **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** — File under **"Financial Fraud"** category. Fill in transaction ID, amount, recipient details, and upload evidence. You'll get an acknowledgment number — save it.

### Step 4 — Lock Aadhaar Biometrics (for AEPS fraud specifically)
- **UIDAI portal/mAadhaar app:** [uidai.gov.in](https://uidai.gov.in) → "Aadhaar Lock/Unlock" → Lock biometrics immediately to prevent further unauthorized AEPS withdrawals.

### Step 5 — Escalate to Banking Ombudsman (if bank doesn't resolve in time)
- **RBI Complaint Management System:** [cms.rbi.org.in](https://cms.rbi.org.in) — if your bank fails to resolve the complaint or refund as per RBI's "limited/zero liability" customer protection rules (usually within 90 days).

### Step 6 — File FIR at Local Police Cyber Cell
- Recommended for larger amounts or when the bank/1930 process doesn't lead to recovery — needed for any further legal/insurance claims.

## 4. Helpful Resources

| Need | Resource |
|---|---|
| **RBI rules on customer liability for fraud** | [rbi.org.in](https://www.rbi.org.in) — covers "zero/limited liability" rules, important to know your rights on refund timelines |
| **Lock/unlock Aadhaar biometrics** | [uidai.gov.in](https://uidai.gov.in) or mAadhaar app — essential for AEPS fraud prevention |
| **UPI fraud-specific complaint** | Most UPI apps (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) have an in-app "Report Transaction" / "Raise Dispute" option — use this alongside bank reporting |
| **NPCI Dispute Redressal** | [npci.org.in](https://www.npci.org.in) — National Payments Corporation of India handles UPI-level dispute escalation if bank/app doesn't resolve |
| **Check account freeze status** | Your bank's app/net banking usually shows real-time status of disputed transactions |

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Call your bank immediately — block card/account, get complaint reference number
2. ✅ Dial 1930 within the golden hour
3. ✅ File complaint on cybercrime.gov.in with transaction ID and evidence
4. ✅ Lock Aadhaar biometrics via UIDAI if AEPS fraud is suspected
5. ✅ Raise an in-app dispute on the UPI/wallet app used
6. ✅ Escalate to RBI Ombudsman (cms.rbi.org.in) if unresolved
7. ✅ File FIR for larger losses or unresolved cases
8. ✅ Change all banking/UPI passwords from a secure device
