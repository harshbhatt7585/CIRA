# Playbook 3: Harassment, Stalking & Threats

**Categories Covered:** Bullying, Stalking, Sexting (non-consensual), Intimidation, Provocative Speech

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

1. **Do not respond or retaliate** to the harasser/stalker — engaging often escalates the behavior and can be used against you later ("provoked them" defense).
2. **Block the person immediately** on all platforms where contact is happening (social media, messaging apps, phone).
3. **Do NOT delete any messages, comments, or content** — even though it's tempting, you need this as evidence. Screenshot first, block after.
4. **Tighten your privacy settings** — make profiles private, restrict who can message/comment/tag you, remove location-sharing from apps.
5. **If it's a sextortion/non-consensual sexting case:**
   - Do **not** pay any ransom/blackmail demand — paying rarely stops the threat and confirms you'll pay again.
   - Do **not** delete the threatening messages — preserve them as evidence.
   - Cover your webcam when not in use, and check if your device/accounts have been compromised (change passwords, enable 2FA).
6. **If there's an immediate physical threat or you feel unsafe** (stalking with real-world contact, threats of violence), **call local police emergency number immediately** — this overrides all other steps.
7. **Inform someone you trust** (family/friend) about the situation so you're not handling it alone, especially in stalking cases where physical safety may be involved.
8. **For workplace/school harassment**, inform HR, your manager, or school counselor in writing (email) so there's a record of you reporting it internally too.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Screenshots of all messages/comments/posts** | Full conversation with visible usernames, timestamps, and platform name |
| **Call/SMS logs** | Screenshot call history showing repeated/unwanted calls, and save voicemails if threats were left |
| **Profile details of the harasser** | Username, profile URL, phone number if known |
| **Location/stalking evidence** | Notes on dates/times/places where you noticed being followed or watched, CCTV footage requests if available nearby |
| **Threatening or explicit content** | Save (don't forward widely) any threatening images/messages/sextortion material — store securely, only share with police/cybercrime portal |
| **Witness accounts** | If others witnessed harassment (in person or online, e.g., in group chats), get their statements/screenshots too |
| **Timeline log** | Chronological record: first incident, escalation pattern, all actions taken (very important for stalking/harassment patterns) |
| **Medical/psychological records** | If the harassment caused stress, anxiety, or harm requiring medical attention, keep those records — useful for legal proceedings |

**Important:** For sextortion/explicit content cases, avoid sharing the content widely even as "evidence" — report directly to the cybercrime portal or platform's dedicated secure reporting tool instead.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Immediate Safety Concern? Call Emergency Services First
- If there is a direct threat to life/safety or in-person stalking, call **112** (India's national emergency number) before anything else.

### Step 2 — Report to the Platform
- **Instagram/Facebook:** Report → "Harassment" or "Bullying" or "Threatening violence"
- **WhatsApp:** Block + Report (sends recent messages to WhatsApp for review)
- **X (Twitter):** [help.x.com/forms/abusiveuser](https://help.x.com/forms/abusiveuser)
- **YouTube:** Report comment/video → "Harassment or bullying"
- Most platforms have a dedicated **"Safety Center"** with priority handling for threats

### Step 3 — Report to National Cybercrime Authorities (India)
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — Use the special **"Report Crime Related to Women/Child"** category for stalking, sextortion, sexting harassment (this gets priority handling and allows anonymous reporting for sensitive cases).
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** for financial sextortion/blackmail cases.
- **Women Helpline:** Dial **181** (national helpline for women in distress)
- **Local Police Cyber Cell / FIR:** File an FIR, especially for stalking (a cognizable offense under IPC/BNS), criminal intimidation, or sextortion.

### Step 4 — National Commission Reporting (gender-based cases)
- **National Commission for Women (NCW):** [ncwapps.nic.in/onlinecomplaintsv2](https://ncwapps.nic.in/onlinecomplaintsv2/) — for women facing harassment/stalking/threats.
- **National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR):** [ncpcr.gov.in](https://ncpcr.gov.in) — if the victim is a minor (POCSO Act applies).
- **POCSO e-Box:** [ncpcr.gov.in/ebox](https://ncpcr.gov.in/ebox/) — anonymous reporting for child sexual abuse/harassment material.

### Step 5 — Report to Sanchar Saathi (for call/SMS-based harassment)
- **Chakshu (Sanchar Saathi):** [sancharsaathi.gov.in](https://www.sancharsaathi.gov.in) — Report harassing calls/SMS from a spoofed or unknown number for action against the telecom connection.

## 4. Helpful Resources

| Need | Resource |
|---|---|
| **Remove non-consensual intimate images (NCII)** | [StopNCII.org](https://stopncii.org) — creates a digital fingerprint (hash) of the image so it gets blocked across partner platforms (Meta, TikTok, etc.) without you uploading the image publicly |
| **Sextortion-specific support** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) (priority category) + [Childline India 1098](https://www.childlineindia.org) if victim is a minor |
| **Mental health support during harassment/stalking** | iCall (TISS) helpline: 9152987821, or Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345 |
| **Legal aid** | [National Legal Services Authority (NALSA)](https://nalsa.gov.in) — free legal aid for harassment/stalking victims |
| **Block & document tool** | Most platforms' built-in "Download Your Data" feature can export full message history as backup evidence |

---

## 5. Quick Checklist Summary

1. ✅ Screenshot everything BEFORE blocking
2. ✅ Block the harasser on all platforms
3. ✅ Don't engage, don't pay, don't delete evidence
4. ✅ Call 112 if there's an immediate safety threat
5. ✅ Report on cybercrime.gov.in (priority category for women/child) → dial 1930/181 as relevant
6. ✅ File FIR for stalking/intimidation/sextortion
7. ✅ Use StopNCII.org if intimate images are involved
8. ✅ Reach out for mental health support — you don't have to handle this alone
