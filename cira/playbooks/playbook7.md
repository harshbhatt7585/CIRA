# Playbook 7: Card & SIM Fraud

**Categories Covered:** Card Fraud, SIM Swap Fraud

---

## 1. Immediate Steps (Stop the Damage & Recover Fast)

### Card Fraud (debit/credit card skimming, cloning, unauthorized online use):
1. **Call your bank's official card helpline immediately** (number from the back of the card or official bank website) to **block the card** — this stops further misuse instantly.
2. **Do not share your card number, CVV, expiry date, or OTP** with anyone, even if they claim to be bank staff verifying the fraud.
3. **Check your transaction history** for the full pattern of unauthorized use — note every suspicious transaction, not just the first one.
4. **Request a replacement card** once blocked, and ensure the new card has a different number (not just reactivated).
5. **Initiate a transaction dispute/chargeback** with your bank immediately — most banks require this within 3-7 days of noticing fraud for "zero liability" protection to apply.
6. **If the physical card was lost/stolen** (not just cloned), report it as lost to the bank in the same call.

### SIM Swap Fraud (someone fraudulently gets a new SIM issued with your number, hijacking OTPs):
1. **Recognize the warning sign**: Your phone suddenly shows **"No Service"** or **"Emergency Calls Only"** when it shouldn't, or you stop receiving calls/SMS unexpectedly — this often means your SIM has just been swapped/deactivated.
2. **Immediately call your telecom provider's customer care** (from another phone) to report unauthorized SIM swap and request immediate deactivation of the fraudulent SIM.
3. **Call your bank immediately** to freeze accounts linked to that phone number — since OTPs now go to the attacker's SIM, they can drain bank/UPI accounts within minutes.
4. **Change passwords for all accounts** linked to that phone number (email, banking, social media) from a different device, as the attacker may already have used the SIM to reset passwords via OTP.
5. **Visit your telecom provider's store in person** with ID proof if phone-based recovery doesn't work, to reclaim your number.
6. **Enable app-based 2FA** (like Google Authenticator) instead of SMS-based OTP wherever possible, going forward, to reduce future risk.

## 2. Evidence Storage (What to Save & Why)

| Evidence Type | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Transaction SMS/alerts** | Screenshot every fraud transaction alert (amount, time, merchant/recipient) |
| **Bank statement** | Screenshot/download the section showing unauthorized card transactions |
| **Card blocking confirmation** | Save SMS/email confirmation from the bank once the card is blocked, with complaint reference number |
| **SIM swap timeline** | Note the exact time service was lost, when you reported it to the telecom provider, and their response/reference number |
| **Telecom provider complaint reference** | Save the ticket/complaint number given when you report the SIM swap |
| **Identity proof of original SIM ownership** | Keep your ID proof, SIM purchase record, or past bills handy — needed to prove the SIM was fraudulently reissued |
| **Linked account fraud evidence** | If bank/social accounts were accessed via OTP after the swap, screenshot those unauthorized changes/transactions too |
| **Timeline log** | Exact sequence: when service was lost → when you called telecom → when you called bank → when you filed complaints |

**Tip:** For SIM swap cases, **speed of documentation matters most** — the attacker usually acts within minutes of getting the new SIM, so a precise timeline helps banks and police trace the fraud window.

## 3. Reporting (In Priority Order)

### Step 1 — Call Your Bank's Card Helpline IMMEDIATELY
- Block the card, request chargeback/dispute, and get a complaint reference number.

### Step 2 — Call Your Telecom Provider (for SIM swap)
- Report the fraudulent SIM swap and request immediate deactivation/reclaiming of your number. Get a complaint/ticket number.

### Step 3 — Dial 1930 (National Cyber Crime Financial Fraud Helpline)
- Report immediately for the fastest chance of freezing the fraudster's account before withdrawal — critical for both card fraud and SIM-swap-enabled banking fraud.

### Step 4 — File a Complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
- **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** — File under "Financial Fraud," including transaction details, card/SIM fraud timeline, and telecom complaint reference.

### Step 5 — Report to Sanchar Saathi (specifically for SIM swap)
- **[sancharsaathi.gov.in](https://www.sancharsaathi.gov.in)** → **TAFCOP** (Know Your Mobile Connections) — check if any other unauthorized SIMs are issued in your name.
- Also report the fraudulent SIM issuance through Sanchar Saathi's complaint/feedback options, in addition to your telecom provider's direct complaint channel.

### Step 6 — Escalate to TRAI/Banking Ombudsman (if unresolved)
- **TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India):** [trai.gov.in](https://www.trai.gov.in) — escalate if your telecom provider doesn't act on the SIM swap complaint.
- **RBI Banking Ombudsman:** [cms.rbi.org.in](https://cms.rbi.org.in) — escalate if your bank doesn't resolve the card fraud dispute satisfactorily.

### Step 7 — File FIR at Local Police Cyber Cell
- Important for larger losses or to support your bank's investigation/insurance claim process.
